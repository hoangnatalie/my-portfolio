Overview

Keeping track of workouts with a notepad and pen is outdated, and paying for apps can be frustrating. Our goal is to create a free app that eliminates paywalls and helps users stay motivated by connecting with friends and family.

Problem

Many apps that offer a way to log workouts and track food intake require in-app purchases. Most people are turned away from that and rely on physical pen and paper or use multiple apps to solve this problem.

Misho the Gym Bro

About

Misho started his fitness journey at the beginning of his college career. Since then, he has stuck to a strict gym schedule and rarely misses a day. With this routine, Misho ensures that he eats healthy and protein-packed meals.

Needs

  • An app to store his workouts.

Goals

  • Be more organized and ditch the memory strategy.

Pain Points

  • Frustrated by the many apps that require payment to use their full app.

Addressing user confusion - Add Workout

During testing, 90% of beta testers did not understand the Add Workout screen. The search bar caused confusion, since our app didnâ€™t have functionality for a search feature on this page. Beta testers overlooked the plus icon which has a modal appear to add an exercise. In response, we implemented the following changes:

  • Replaced the search bar with input for the workout name.
  • Added a note for users when there are no exercises created.
  • Changed button title to be more specific and disabled it unless an exercise has been added.
